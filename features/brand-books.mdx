## Overview

Brand Books let you store your organization's voice, tone, writing style, and vocabulary in one place. Once created, a Brand Book can be applied to any Writer generation to keep content on-brand automatically.

## What a Brand Book Contains

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| Voice & Tone | How your brand "sounds" — formal, casual, authoritative, friendly, etc. |
| Writing Style | Sentence structure preferences, technicality level, use of jargon |
| Vocabulary | Preferred terms, terms to avoid, brand-specific language |
| Industry | Your industry vertical (used for context-aware generation) |
| Target Audience | Who you're writing for |

## Creating a Brand Book

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="File Upload">
    Upload an existing brand guidelines document in **PDF or DOCX** format. EssayGen extracts the relevant voice and style information automatically.

    1. Go to **Brand Books** in the sidebar
    2. Click **New Brand Book → Upload File**
    3. Upload your PDF or DOCX
    4. Review the extracted guidelines and confirm
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Manual Entry">
    Fill in each field directly in the Brand Book form.

    1. Go to **Brand Books** in the sidebar
    2. Click **New Brand Book → Manual Entry**
    3. Complete the Voice, Style, Vocabulary, Industry, and Audience fields
    4. Click **Save**
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Import">
    Import from a URL or paste raw text from an existing style guide.

    1. Go to **Brand Books** in the sidebar
    2. Click **New Brand Book → Import**
    3. Paste your text or enter a URL
    4. Review and save
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Setting a Default Brand Book

If you have multiple Brand Books (e.g., for different clients or product lines), you can set one as the default. It's automatically selected when you open the Writer.

1. Go to **Brand Books**
2. Click the **⋯** menu on a Brand Book card
3. Select **Set as Default**

## Editing and Archiving

- **Edit** a Brand Book at any time by clicking into it and updating any field
- **Archive** a Brand Book to hide it from the selection list without deleting it (**⋯ → Archive**)

<Tip>
  The more specific your vocabulary and "terms to avoid" sections, the more consistent the output. Include real examples of good vs. bad brand copy if possible.
</Tip>
